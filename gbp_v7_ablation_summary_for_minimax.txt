GBP v7 Ablation Summary — Minimax Input
=======================================

This summary is based on the frozen GBP v7 framework and its ablation script.
Reference files used:
- gbp_complete_v7.py
- gbp_v7_ablation.py

Core conclusion:
The ablation study argues that GBP v7 is not being carried by hidden tuning knobs. The main performance gains come from structural ingredients: boundary scaling, topology, and direct geometric coupling. The obvious overfitting suspects—particle-specific overrides and small correction terms—have much smaller impact on total fit quality.

Main findings:

1. Load-bearing ingredients (large delta MAPE)
- lam / boundary scaling: +5.35% delta MAPE when removed
- Constituent sum only: +5.57% delta MAPE relative to full model
- Force all baryons to S1/T1 topology: +2.63% delta MAPE
- Remove dg term (geo_factor coupling): +1.30% delta MAPE

Interpretation:
- Boundary scaling is doing most of the work. This is a core physics term, not a cosmetic correction.
- The near-match between “remove lam” and “constituent sum only” shows that lam is the primary correction beyond the constituent baseline.
- Topology assignments are essential. T1/T2/T3 is not decorative labeling.
- Direct geometric coupling through dg is real and necessary.

2. DeepSeek “hidden knobs” objection addressed
- Remove geo_factor overrides: only +0.15% delta MAPE overall
- Remove photon branch: +0.28% delta MAPE overall
- Remove gc term: only +0.026% delta MAPE overall

Interpretation:
- The geo_factor overrides are not load-bearing. The fit survives without them.
- The photon branch is not a global crutch. Its overall effect is modest, but it matters strongly where it is supposed to matter.
- gc is a real correction term, but it is small. It should be described honestly as a refinement, not the engine of the model.

3. Localized photon-branch effect
- Removing the photon branch hurts Xi_c*+ and Xi_c*0 strongly: about +5.66% error on those states specifically.

Interpretation:
- This behavior is what a real specialized structural mechanism should look like.
- Small global effect, large local effect where the topology says it should matter.
- That argues for a topological rule rather than generic overfitting.

4. Killer anti-overfitting point
- Removing overrides only meaningfully affects about 5 baryons
- Total overall damage is only +0.15% MAPE

Interpretation:
- If the model were being held up by hidden knobs, removing the overrides should collapse the fit.
- It does not.
- The structure is carrying the model, not patch corrections.

Clean scientific wording:
“The ablation study shows that model performance is driven mainly by boundary scaling, topology, and direct geometric coupling. By contrast, particle-specific overrides contribute only marginally to overall fit quality, which argues against the idea that the result is being carried by hidden tuning knobs.”

Stronger public wording:
“If the model were overfit, removing the special-case overrides should wreck the fit. It doesn’t. What actually hurts the model is removing boundary scaling, topology, and geometric coupling. That points to structural physics, not cosmetic tuning.”

One-line takeaway:
The dominant improvement comes from structural terms, not ad hoc refinements.
